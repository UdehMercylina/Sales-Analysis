# Sales-Analysis


This project represents a shift from simply analyzing data to thinking in terms of systems, performance, and impact—which is central to to data-focused fields.

The core objective is to understand why a business is making or losing money, using transactional sales data as the foundation for analysis.

The dataset captures customer activity, product categories, revenue, profit, discounts, and sales volume across multiple years. Rather than treating it as a simple analysis task, it was approached as a problem of extracting decision-relevant insights from structured data.

         OBJECTIVES & APPROACH
The dataset was cleaned and prepared to ensure consistency before analysis. Exploration focused on identifying patterns in sales performance, evaluating profitability, and examining how pricing strategies influence outcomes.

Key areas of analysis included product-level performance, yearly trends, and the relationship between discounts and profit margins. Visualizations were used to highlight patterns and support interpretation.

             Key Insights
High revenue does not always translate to high profit
Discounting can reduce profitability despite increasing sales volume
A small number of categories drive most of the profit
Profit margins vary significantly across products
